Example 5 - 2-coin algorithm

\[ f(p) = \frac{c_1p_1}{c_1p_1+c_2p_2}, \qquad c_1, c_2 \geq 0 \] When using the substitution \(p_i = \frac{q_1}{q_1+q_{i+1}}, \qquad i \in \{1,2,\ldots,m\}\) (tossing all coins to produce an \(m+2\) sided die), one gets \[ f_{1}(\boldsymbol{q}) \propto (c_1q_1^2+c_1q_1q_3, c_2q_1^2+c_2q_1q_2) \] Instead, when substituing using \(p_i = \frac{q_i}{1-\sum_{k=1}^{i-1}q_k}, \qquad i \in \{1,2,\ldots,m\}\) (tossing coins until first heads to produce an \(m+1\) sided die), one gets \[ f_2(\boldsymbol{q}) \propto (c_1q_1 -c_1q_1^2, c_2q_2) \]
